================================================================================
  INTERPRETATION GUIDE
================================================================================

Significance codes:  *** p<0.001   ** p<0.01   * p<0.05   n.s. not significant

FAVORABLE RESPONSE (≥ 4):
  A rating of 4 or 5 on the 1–5 Likert scale indicates the participant/agent
  perceived that aspect positively.  Reporting proportions makes the results
  accessible: "60% of CROWD participants rated satisfaction favorably."

KRUSKAL–WALLIS:
  Non-parametric analogue of one-way ANOVA.  Tests whether at least one of the
  4 groups (CROWD + 3 agents) draws from a different distribution.  Does NOT
  assume normality — appropriate for ordinal Likert-scale data.  A significant
  result only tells you *some* groups differ, not which ones.

WILCOXON RANK-SUM (= MANN–WHITNEY U):
  Follow-up pairwise test comparing two groups at a time.  Bonferroni correction
  multiplies each p-value by 6 (the number of pairs) to guard against false
  positives from multiple testing.  A significant result means the two groups'
  rating distributions are unlikely to arise from the same population.

WHY PER-PERSONA AVERAGES (n=65)?
  Each LLM agent was simulated 5 times per persona.  Using the raw 325
  observations would inflate statistical power artificially (pseudo-replication).
  Taking the per-persona average first yields one independent observation per
  persona, matching the 65 CROWD participants for a fair comparison.
